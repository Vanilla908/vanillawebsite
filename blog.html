December 29, 2025: My EPIC trip to Hong Kong!

For the first time, I went out of the country on Christmas break to go and visit Hong Kong and Macao! We went and visited a culture I've never seen before and even if me and my family had the worst of Culture shocks, I must say that the region is one of the most marvelous places I've ever visited. The first time I landed at the HKIA, I was greeted with a cleaner and more high-tech environment compared to the international airport of my homeland. When we got to the arrival gate, the entire family was hungry, so we started looking for a restaurant at the airport. We saw a huge Mcdonalds sign and thought that it was a real chain. Turns out, it was just a large prop set for anyone who wants to take a picture of a classic 1990s Hong Kong Mcdonalds. Not only was that a funny and great first impression of Hong Kong, but it is also a sign that I may probably have to learn the new customs and traditions here in this country

Our first obstacle was trying to navigate via their public transport. It isn't bad or anything. All bus stops and routes are clearly labelled with signs and they use electronic cards to make payment much easier for us. The only problem is that we are so used to the weird Jeepney system here in the Philippines that finding a much more efficient way of transport is such a hassle when we have the habits you get when travelling in a third world country. For example, bus stops here have clearly designated stops, and you can't just stop anywhere in between stations. You also just cannot cross roads because in regions outside of Southeast asia, Jaywalking is a crime! I also had to brace myself whenever my Mom asks a question to the locals because:

1. We could not speak their language

2. Filipinos are too dangerously friendly of a human race, so seeing how other people outside of our country actually behave with strangers may shock my mom, and

3. Asian countries, including my own, don't like being asked questions.lol

I understand that this is behavior linked more towards our differences in culture and less about any form of malice, but it doesn't minimize the cringe I get when she starts talking to a local. It's a good thing that in most cases, they are willing to help us out and get stuff for us done despite most of them being busy doing work. I want to try and apologize for the added problems we give them, but I know it will only make their planned work/schedule much harder.

It's a good thing that I am good with directions, can read the english labelled directly below the chinese characters in all signs, and memorize stuff that we need to do later in the day. I basically acted like some demi-tour guide that is also visiting these places for the first time. But despite it all, we went to multiple places around the region! On our first day, we went to Hong Kong Disneyland. I ate the worst burger there in spaceland (why was the bread RED?) and saw a cool parade and drone show there. Not much to see here that you cannot see when opening disney+ , but it only goes up from here.

On our second day, we went to the Ngong Ping 360, a cable car ride that took use to a buddhist temple with a big Buddha statue. We met a lot of south asians here as well that were just as friendly and accomodating as the locals, and we also looked at the grand set piece here in this landmark: a big buddha statue sitting on top of a mountain. It is the most majectic sights I've ever seen, especially when looking at it from the view of the cable car. It might be my favorite place in the entire trip, and I was willing to visit this place one more time if we had hours to spare. Then on the afternoon, we headed to hong kong island and rode a tram car up this steep hill, which gives us a good view of Hong kong's cityscape. This is where I was first exposed to Hong Kong's many shops and businesses, which had very tempting products to buy from. It was also here that I ate some steamed soy chicken, mushroom and rice which is a new favorite dish for me.

On the third day, we visited Tsim Sha Tsui. We rode a bunch of ferries and yachts just to cross the waters between Tsim sha Tsui and Hong Kong itself. All the while, we visited the Avenue of Stars, took a photo in front of the bruce lee statue there, and admired the light show that happened on the evening. After eating this declicious waffle dessert they were serving in the Kowloon public pier, we took a tour bus around Hong Kong and saw a lot of public spaces that may catch my eye like the Ladies Market in Mong kok. We were having a good time, but realized that it is almost closing hours for the MTR line, and we need the train to bring us back to our hotel. We had to quickly leave Hong Kong to end the day, and we are lucky to catch the last train home.

On our final day before depature, my mom decided that we should catch a ferry to Macao. We rode a bus and saw more iconic spots like the Macao Tower, the Guan Yin Statue, their science center, and multiple themed hotels. If I have one impression I've gotten about Macao, it is China's version of Las Vegas. They have their own mini Eiffel Tower, Roman Coliseum, and Big Ben while I was visiting the place. It is also the one place where Jaywalking is allowed (yay crimes!), and for our trip back to our hotel in Hong kong, we took a highway that crossed the Freakin sea. I just had to admire the people who has the audacity to build someting this wide in the middle of the freakin sea.

We went back to the Philippines on our fifth day, and this is where I wrap up my thoughts about the trip. I don't want people to think that this is just mindless glazing of China because despite its highs, there are some lows that can be identified when you look further down their history. However, as a tourist spot, it definitely nailed the Urban Holiday vibes I wanna look forward to during Christmas season. The locals are accomodating and are really good at their job. They are never late for anything like arriving at a bus stop or train station, and there are a lot of engineering marvels to be seen when going to Macao and Hong Kong. To those wishing for a quieter and simpler environment, the Ngong Ping 360 may just be the place for you, and there are also other spots that I have not visited that are just beautiful densely packed forests. The only thing that got me sour is that 5 days may be too long of a vacation trip, and by the third day, I was feeling a little bit homesick lol. Thankfully, we arrived at Manila safely, and the first thing I was greeted to was the hot scorching heat of the sun and the heavy traffic just outside of Newport City. How i missed this lol.

May 14, 2025: CAD-CAM, The Batangas Province, and Research

I am still going to be making an HM Parody animation at some point, but I'll probably make fun of it while making the script for it lel. Anyway, my addiction in this time of year is learning about computer aided design and manufacturing; specifically, studying about 3D Printing in dentistry and its associated programs and software. It only came about when I did a project in school wherein I made a group presentation about 3D Printing Resin in Dental CAD. I was already in a dentistry course, and my father introduced me to CAD way before, but I wasn't technically having fun studying it because information about it was pretty scarce, and I was a brat that did not like learning new things. Fast forward to today, and I am now responsible for carrying a group report in which I voluntarily proposed the topic to all of my 4 members that did not know anything about CAD or even 3D Printing, so I had to do all of the research, script, and powerpoint design on my own. It was hard and confusing at first getting to relearn the basics of 3D Printing again, but once I got deeper into the subject, I began to have fun teaching myself this modern workflow. I guess back then, I was scared by the user interfaces of the different dental CAD Programs out there like blueskybio, but now that I've experienced more complicated workflows like building a website with Javascript code, I can now see that Dental CAD is just a walk in the park. So when I presented my report to the entire class, my friends and classmates were interested in the many things I know about this complicated subject. When I finally completed my report, I got questions from starry-eyed colleagues asking me more about how the Dental CAD workflow works, and I happily answered all of them. I enjoyed teaching them about it, but I was also inspired by their curiosity and drive to know something that I, in the past, would disregard as nothing more than a fad. So as person with a newfound passion to teach people this skill that they may need in the future, I went about and learned more about CAD-CAM; I looked into the latest 3D Printers, Resins that may be biocompatible within the oral cavity, and reviewed blue sky bio's website to learn more about their software's modules. I am going to be livestreaming myself learning about blue sky bio's features, so if you are in the medical field and want to learn how you can incorporated 3D printing into your workflow, I suggest going to my youtube channel!

Aside from that, I've also been strrugling with Math (Oh no!). So when I was in school, my biostatistics teacher had us do a sort of mini-research paper with a group. This group was different from my CAD report group, but they are cooperative enough to fill out the review of related literature, the abstract, the discussion, and the introduction to our paper. However, when it came down to analyzing and calculating the results, no one was willing to take the plunder. There was technically someone in charge of writing the results section of our academic research, but the guy wasn't posting any of his calculations. When it finally reached the deadline, no one still hasn't done the maths, so I took it upon myself to record each one of the 120 respondents' raw results and calculate them via this formula given to me by my professor to measure correlation between two variables. I had to do this twice because of how much data there was, but eventually I got the answers that my group desparately needs. However, when reviewing the results, I realized that I made an error. Upon fixing it, which took a long time, a thought crossed my mind: what if I got the other calculatons wrong? So to substantitate my paranoia, I did the calculations twice more while reviewing the data sets that I inputted. I just got done finishing it this morning, and I dropped to my bed while trying to ignore the fear that maybe I still have some mismatched answers in my excel sheet.

Anyway, once I was done with all of my school work in my dental course, I had a long summer break ahead of me. To start off my vacation, I went to a mini-resort in Batangas wherein my family celebrated one of my lolas' birthday. We had a pretty big buffet which is a practice for every filipino celebration, and I got sick trying to eat some empanadas provided by the waiters. October 31, 2024: Candlewick, Failing to Help Someone Cheat, and Others

One day, my professor in university announced a pop quiz for us to study in oral pathology. Like any pop quizzes, the classmates (including me) are not prepared for the quiz. It shocked everyone, including the 2 persons sitting next to me. However, the teacher was nice and gave us 2 hours to study before the exam. My seatmate scoot over to me and whispered in my ear. People knew me as the smart person in the room. While I do not get the best grades in class, it is most likely guaranteed that I pass all of my quizzes and exams. My seatmate knew this, so he came up to me and asked, "Hey, can you help me cheat on the exam?" Me, being afraid to say no to the guys because I do not want to get into their bad side, reluctantly said yes to the request. But in the back of my mind, I was figuring out how I should get out of this situation. The teacher can just catch us if we are not careful, and even if I'm not the one cheating, I may be charged for helping someone cheat. Luckily for me, a series of wacky events happened that made sure that I did not help them in the long run.

However, the teacher announced right before the quiz to distance ourselves with each other. I was positioned at the back of the class while she announced this, and my bad hearing thought that the announcement was directed toward the three of us, only for me to realize after the exam that it was an announcement for the entire class. It could have been anyone that moved, but my dumb brain interpreted it as a signal for me to move to another location away from the two idiots I have by my side.
